State of the art tool that realistically simulates various optical components and provides a suit of low level and high-level quantum networking protocols which can be directly plugged into their applications by users. Why do you need a Quantum Networks Simulator?

Quantum is future!

There is a lot of interest in the Quantum Networking domain. New algorithms and protocols are being developed in various academic and corporate settings. Experimental verification of these will need very expensive setups to be implemented. Setting up a basic network in itself is a very challenging task. We, through this Quantum Network Simulator aim to alleviate the cost barrier of entry so that researchers can prototype and test protocols. We also aim at allowing scalable network architectures to be implemented so that researchers can study the impact of their algorithms on huge graphs. Research in other areas of Quantum Computing also require entangled states to be distributed between any two nodes in the network. In a nutshell, we provide the users with a full-blown Quantum Communication Infrastructure that can be used to prototype protocols as well as used as a base of other applications that depend on quantum state distribution between multiple nodes.

What are Quantum Networks anyways?

Quantum networking is an emerging field which aims at harnessing the properties of light to distribute quantum information and enable fields such as secure communications etc. Quantum Networks consist of nodes capable of sending/receiving quantum information and optical channels capable of carrying this information. The current goal is robust implementation of these networks and make them capable of serving applications that can take advantage of quantum data transmission.
